SOCIAL MEDIA DO'S AND DON'TS FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS

--- THE 5 DO'S ---

Do treat your profile like a living resume. Your old-school reputation matters just as much in the digital age, so keep it clean for future employers.
Do lock down your privacy settings. Keep your private life private—some traditions of boundaries never go out of style.
Do fact-check before you share. We're forward-thinking scholars, not out here spreading baseless rumors.
Do network purposefully. Use platforms to actually build your future and connect with industry veterans.
Do take a breath before replying. If a post triggers you, step away. The drafts folder is your absolute best friend.

--- THE 5 DON'TS ---

Don't post anything you wouldn't want your grandparents to see. Seriously, no sugar-coating it: the internet is forever.
Don't badmouth your university, professors, or peers. Have some traditional respect; screenshots exist and word travels ridiculously fast.
Don't overshare your real-time location. Basic safety first—don't broadcast exactly where you are to the whole grid.
Don't engage in random comment wars. It's a massive waste of energy and just leaves a messy digital footprint.
Don't rely on "vanishing" messages. People screenshot everything. Assume absolutely nothing is truly temporary.
